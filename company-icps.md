# The ICPs for The Snow Media

##   **ICP 1 — Home Service Businesses (Top Priority)**

Core Profile

* Plumbing, HVAC, Electrical, Roofing, Garage Doors

* Revenue: **$1M–$50M**

* Owner still involved in operations

Buying Triggers (strongest “buy now” signals)

* Lead flow inconsistent month-to-month

* High dependence on HomeAdvisor/Angi (margin killer)

* Looking to expand service area or add another truck

* Competitors outranking them in local search and ads

* Seasonality hurting cash flow

Pain Points You Can Solve (direct ROI clarity)

* High CPAs on Google because campaigns aren’t structured correctly

* Spending money on irrelevant search terms

* Poor landing pages → low conversion rates

* “Slow-to-respond” leads due to weak automation

* No tracking → they don’t know what campaigns actually work

* Overpaying for low-quality shared leads

Buying Committee

* Owner

* Ops Manager (sometimes)

* Office Manager (influences lead handling)

What They Value

* Immediate lead volume

* Lower cost per booked job

* Clear reporting and attribution

* Simple communication

* No fluff, just booked calls

Disqualifiers

* No CRM

* No call tracking

* Owner unwilling to update website or landing pages

* Only low-ticket jobs (\<$200 avg ticket)

**Why this ICP should dominate your next 90 days:**  
 Fastest yes/no decisions, smaller buying committee, clear ROI, and your $2k retainer fits their expectations perfectly.

## **ICP 2 — E-Commerce Brands (Secondary Priority)**

Larger upside but longer cycles and more sophisticated expectations.

Core Profile

* DTC brands with **$1M–$30M** in annual revenue

* Mostly Google \+ Meta focused

Buying Triggers

* ROAS decline

* MER decline

* Heavy dependence on branded traffic

* Seasonal spikes approaching

* Investors pressuring for scale

Pain Points You Solve

* Feed quality issues hurting Shopping ROAS

* Under-tested creative/angles on Meta

* No real attribution or unified reporting

* Over-segmented campaigns limiting signal strength

* Overreliance on discounting

* Weak retargeting and lifecycle setup

* No structured testing roadmap

* Review LTV / CPA ratio for further scaling and NCA  
* Conversion tracking inconsistencies and poor setup

Buying Committee

* Co / Founder / CEO

* Head of Growth / CMO  
  * Director of Marketing, Brand & Marketing Director

* Sometimes COO

What They Value

* Professional reporting

* Clear optimization plan

* A 90-day performance roadmap

* Competency in both Meta and Google

* Communication that cuts through complexity

Disqualifiers

* Under $1M/year

* Low-margin products

* No PMF

* Founder who wants “cheap” results

**Reality check:**  
 This ICP is higher upside but will demand more case studies, more structure, and more proof.

## **ICP 3 — Consulting / Professional Services (Third Priority)**

Longer cycles, higher friction, but good retainers long-term.

Core Profile

* High-ticket consultants and B2B service firms

* Selling **$10k–$250k**\+ offers

* Typically referral-driven

* Want to add paid acquisition to their pipeline

Buying Triggers

* Unpredictable deal flow

* Weak positioning

* Landing pages not converting

* Over-reliance on referrals

* Irregular top-of-funnel activity

* Bogus / unqualified leads

* Low lead volume

Pain Points You Solve

* Poor targeting → wrong leads

* No messaging clarity

* No nurture or retargeting system

* Long sales cycle with no pipeline consistency

* Outdated landing pages \+ no CRO

* Low volume of SQLs

Buying Committee

* Founder / CEO

* COO

* Marketing Director (if they have one)

* Principal Consultant

What They Value

* Pipeline stability

* Shorter sales cycles

* Clean messaging

* Qualified inbound traffic

* Strategic thinking

* SQLs growth and high-value leads

Disqualifiers

* No offer clarity

* No sales process

* Founder not involved in sales or operations

* Unwilling to invest 90+ days before seeing full ROI
